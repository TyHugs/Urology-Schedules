Individual View — Four Schedule Tables
Understanding the week-by-day grid for each data source

Each provider's data is shown in a 5-week × 5-day grid (Mon–Fri). Up to four tables display simultaneously:

Table 1 — OR Schedule: Operating room assignments, including room number, facility (UH, BCSC, etc.), and whether the block is FCFS (First-Come, First-Served).

Table 2 — Clinic Template: Scheduled clinic sessions by location (Taubman, BCSC, Northville, etc.) with AM/PM breakdowns. Pilot data for select providers.

Table 3 — QGenda Schedule: Shifts and assignments from QGenda, color-coded by activity type.

Blue — OR / Procedure Green — Clinic Purple — Admin / Research Orange — Call / IR

Table 4 — RVU Production: Actual work RVU activity (Nov '25–Jan '26), showing average wRVUs, sample size, and primary activity/location per cell.

Tip: Hover over any cell chip for additional detail. Empty cells indicate no data was found in that source for that day and week.
Compare View
Side-by-side comparison of multiple providers

Select two or more providers from the search bar to compare their schedules side by side. Each provider's four tables are displayed in their own column, making it easy to spot coverage gaps, overlapping OR blocks, or differences in clinical vs. administrative time across providers.

Tip: This is particularly useful for division chiefs evaluating coverage balance or planning staffing adjustments.
Department View
Bird's-eye view of all providers by division

The Department view shows every provider in a single overview, organized by division. Each provider's row displays a condensed week summary from their QGenda data, allowing you to quickly scan for patterns, gaps, and capacity across the entire department.

Use the Division dropdown to filter to a specific division. Click any provider's name to jump to their full Individual view.

Discrepancies Panel
Cross-source conflicts and data quality issues

The Discrepancies tab automatically detects conflicts between the four data sources. Each issue is flagged with a severity level:

High: Providers scheduled as "Admin" in QGenda but generating patient-facing RVUs — potential scheduling errors requiring immediate attention.

Medium: Providers missing from QGenda entirely or showing activity-type mismatches between sources.

Low: Location discrepancies or minor labeling differences between QGenda and RVU records.

Use the severity and type filters at the top to focus on the most actionable items. Click any provider name to view their full schedule.

Tip: Start with High-severity items — these represent the biggest opportunities to correct scheduling errors and recapture clinical capacity.
Schedule Advisor (AI-Powered)
Ask natural language questions about schedules and capacity

The Advisor tab lets you ask plain-language questions about your department's scheduling data. The AI analyzes all four data sources and returns structured, actionable insights.

Example questions you can ask:

→"If Kennedy increases FTE from 0.5 to 1.0, how many sessions can she add?"
→"Which Oncology providers have the most discrepancies?"
→"Who has open capacity on Wednesdays?"
→"Compare Hadj-Moussa and Bechard's clinic utilization"

Click any of the example chips below the input box to try a pre-built question. The Advisor automatically gathers the relevant data for the providers and divisions mentioned in your question.

Tip: The more specific your question, the more precise the analysis. Mention provider names, divisions, or days of the week for the best results.
About the Data
Source details, coverage, and known limitations

OR Schedule: 40 providers with block assignments. Source: department OR schedule spreadsheet.

Clinic Template: Pilot data for 5 providers (Bechard, Blair, Collin, Ellimoottil, Hadj-Moussa). Additional provider files will be added in future phases.

QGenda: 67 providers with shift-level data including activity type and location codes.

RVU Production: 67 providers with 3-month rolling averages (Nov 2025–Jan 2026). Reflects actual clinical activity volume.

Note: This is a Phase 1 build. Clinic template data is being expanded, and discrepancy rules are being refined. If you notice incorrect data, please flag it for the development team.
